Eastern Music...

Eastern music refers to the diverse musical traditions and styles of the Eastern world, including the Middle East, South Asia, East Asia, and Southeast Asia. It encompasses a wide range of genres, from traditional folk music and classical music to contemporary popular music.



History

Eastern music history is a vast and complex subject, as it encompasses the musical traditions of many different regions and cultures throughout Asia. The earliest known forms of Eastern music can be traced back to ancient civilizations such as China, India, and Persia. In China, for example, traditional music dates back over 3,000 years and was originally used in religious rituals and ceremonies In India, classical music has been practiced for over 2,000 years and is closely tied to the country's spiritual and cultural traditions. Over time, various musical styles and instruments developed throughout Eastern cultures. For example, the classical music of Japan is heavily influenced by the traditional court music of China, while the music of Indonesia incorporates elements of Indian and Arab music. Throughout history, Eastern music has also been influenced by the interactions between different cultures and civilizations.
